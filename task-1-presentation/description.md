# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used
Canva (canva.com) – a free, browser-based graphic design platform

## About the Infographic
The infographic is titled "Digital Literacy in the Modern Age" and covers
five key topics relevant to college students:

1. **What is Digital Literacy?** – Defining the concept of using digital
   tools safely, responsibly and effectively in everyday life.

2. **Useful Digital Tools for Students** – Highlighting platforms like
   GitHub, LinkedIn, Canva, Google Workspace and HackerRank that help
   students learn, collaborate and build careers.

3. **Safe Internet Practices** – Covering password safety, two-factor
   authentication, and avoiding suspicious links.

4. **Cybercrime Awareness** – Introducing common threats like phishing
   and UPI fraud, with reporting channels cybercrime.gov.in and helpline 1930.

5. **Professional Online Presence** – Emphasising the importance of
   maintaining a professional and positive digital identity on platforms
   like GitHub and LinkedIn.

## Reflection
I used Canva to create this infographic as it offered a wide range of
free, professionally designed templates that required no prior design
experience. The template I chose had a clean blue and purple colour
scheme with distinct sections and icons, making it easy to organise
the content visually.

The most interesting part of this task was discovering how much thought
goes into visual communication — choosing the right layout, colours, and
icons to convey information clearly without overwhelming the viewer. The
most challenging aspect was fitting all five topics onto a single page
while keeping the text concise and readable. This task gave me a new
appreciation for design thinking and how visuals can make complex
information far more accessible than plain text alone.
