# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India



##  General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — combine uppercase,
   lowercase, numbers and symbols. Never reuse the same password across
   multiple platforms.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts
   including email, GitHub, LinkedIn, and banking apps.

3. **Never click on suspicious links** received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email —
   even if the message appears to come from a known contact or official brand.

4. **Verify websites before entering any personal information** — always check
   that the URL starts with https:// and look for the padlock icon in the
   browser.

5. **Do not share personal details publicly** — avoid posting your phone number,
   Aadhaar number, date of birth, or home address on social media.

6. **Keep your devices and apps updated** — software updates often contain
   security patches that protect against the latest threats.



##  UPI & Financial Safety (Mandatory Tips)

7. **Never approve an incoming collect request on UPI unless you initiated it**
   — a refund or cashback will NEVER come as a collect request. Approving one
   means money LEAVES your account, not enters it.

8. **Never search for customer care numbers on Google** — always find helpline
   numbers inside the official app or on the company's official website.
   Scammers post fake numbers on websites that rank high in search results.

9. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — not even with
   someone claiming to be a bank employee, RBI officer, or customer care agent.
   No legitimate official will ever ask for these.

10. **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking app to minimise losses
    in case of fraud.

---

##  Social Media & Identity Safety

11. **Review your privacy settings regularly** on Instagram, Facebook, and
    Snapchat — ensure your profile, posts, and stories are visible only to
    people you trust.

12. **Do not accept follow or friend requests from strangers** — fake profiles
    are commonly used for phishing, blackmail, and identity theft.

---

##  If You Are a Victim – How to Report

If you experience any cybercrime including UPI fraud, phishing, cyberbullying,
or identity theft, report it immediately through the following official channels:

-  **National Cyber Crime Portal:** cybercrime.gov.in
-  **Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930 (available 24x7, toll-free)
-  **For UPI fraud:** Also call your bank's helpline immediately to freeze
  the transaction

> The sooner you report, the higher the chance of recovering lost funds.
