# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study

## Topic: UPI/Online Payment Fraud


## Case Study: The Fake Customer Care Scam

### What is UPI Fraud?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where
attackers trick victims into transferring money or revealing sensitive payment
credentials through deception and social engineering.



### How It Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. A college student named Priya recently started using a food delivery app and
   faced an issue with a failed payment of ₹350.
2. She searched for the app's customer care number on Google. Unknown to her,
   scammers had posted a fake customer care number on a fraudulent website that
   appeared in the search results.
3. She called the number. The person who answered sounded professional and asked
   for her UPI ID and registered mobile number to "process the refund."
4. The scammer then sent a collect request of ₹1 on her UPI app, telling her to
   "approve it to verify your account."
5. Priya approved the request without reading it carefully. The scammer
   immediately sent a second collect request for ₹15,000 which Priya mistakenly
   approved, believing it was the refund coming in.
6. Within seconds, ₹15,000 was debited from her account.



### Who is Usually Targeted?
- First-time UPI users and college students
- People who search for customer care numbers on Google instead of official apps
- Anyone who has recently faced a transaction failure and is anxious to recover money



### Consequences
- Immediate financial loss with little chance of recovery
- Emotional distress and loss of trust in digital payment systems
- In some cases, victims share OTPs leading to complete account compromise
- Academic disruption as students may lose money meant for fees or essentials

Fictional scenario based on common UPI fraud patterns reported by the
